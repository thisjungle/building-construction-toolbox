Building and Construction General On-site Award 2020

Last Update: August 2024

Who Does This Apply To?

This award covers a wide range of businesses and workers in the building and construction industry. This includes companies that do:

  • General building work (like houses and offices)
  • Civil construction (like roads and bridges)
  • Metal and engineering construction

If you have employees who are:

  • Labourers
  • Tradespeople (like carpenters and bricklayers)
  • People who operate machinery on site
  • Labour hire workers placed with construction companies

This award likely applies to them.

What About Pay?

The award sets the minimum amount you need to pay your employees. There are different pay levels depending on:

  • The job they do
  • Their skills and qualifications

For example, a general labourer might have a different minimum wage than a qualified carpenter.

There are also extra payments called allowances that might apply in certain situations:

  • Leading hand allowance for employees in charge of a team
  • Some jobs in the residential building sector might have rates that already include industry or tool allowances

Working Hours and Overtime

While the award doesn't specifically say what standard hours are, it does have rules about overtime. Higher rates of pay apply for:

  • Working more than usual hours on weekdays
  • Working on Saturdays, Sundays, or public holidays

For example:

  • Overtime on weekdays: 1.5x normal rate for first couple of hours, then 2x rate after that
  • Sundays and public holidays: Usually have even higher penalty rates

Leave Entitlements

The award ensures employees get time off through various leave types:

  • Annual leave (paid holidays)
  • Personal/carer's leave (for sickness or caring for family)
  • Compassionate leave (for bereavement)
  • Parental leave

Important Updates

The award gets updated from time to time. The most recent update was in August 2024, which included:

  • Updates to definitions
  • Rules around casual employment
  • New "right to disconnect" rule (employees don't have to respond to work calls/emails outside work hours)

Who Does This Award Apply To?

This award applies to a wide range of businesses and the people they employ in the building and construction world. This includes companies involved in:

General Building and Construction

This is your everyday construction work, like building houses, apartments, offices, and shops. It also covers renovations, repairs, demolitions, and even landscaping work done as part of a building project.

Example: A company that builds new homes or renovates existing ones, and their employees like carpenters, bricklayers, and general labourers, would likely be covered.

Civil Construction

This involves bigger projects like roads, bridges, tunnels, railways, water and sewerage systems, and even sports stadiums.

Example: A company constructing a new highway or a team working on building a bridge would fall under this category.

Metal and Engineering Construction

This covers large industrial and commercial projects, including factories, power plants, and installing things like lifts and escalators.

Example: A business that fabricates and installs steel structures for a warehouse, and their boilermakers and welders, would likely be covered.

Specific Worker Types

Specifically, if you employ people who work on-site as:

  • Labourers
  • Trade workers (like carpenters, plumbers, electricians, painters, bricklayers, and roof tilers)
  • Machine operators (like excavators and forklifts)

This award is likely relevant. Even if you use a labour hire company to get workers for your construction projects, those workers are also covered by this award.

Important Note

There are some exceptions. For example, managers and some administrative staff might not be covered by this specific award. There might be other awards that apply to different types of work within the broader industry, such as the Timber Award or the Electrical Award. If you're unsure, it's always best to check the full award details or get advice.
